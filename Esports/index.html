Esports Betting Guide: Everything You Need to Know

Welcome to your ultimate guide for esports betting! Whether you're a newbie or a seasoned bettor in the world of esports, this guide will provide all the knowledge you need to make informed, strategic betting decisions.

From selecting the best esports sportsbooks to understanding betting strategies and promotions, we’ll help you maximize your chances of success in esports betting.

What is Esports Betting?

Esports betting is the practice of placing wagers on the outcome of competitive video game events. Esports has grown exponentially in recent years, drawing massive audiences and offering thrilling betting opportunities with popular games like:

League of Legends

Dota 2

CS:GO

Valorant

In Esports betting, you can place wagers on match results, specific player performances, in-game events, or even long-term outcomes like tournament winners.

Understanding the mechanics of esports betting will help you become a successful bettor in this fast-paced, exciting world.

Types of Esports Bets

Esports betting offers a wide range of betting options, allowing fans to wager on competitive gaming events.

These bets are similar to traditional sports betting but often come with unique types of wagers that cater to esports’ fast-paced and strategic nature. Here’s a breakdown of the most popular types of bets in esports betting:

  • Match Winner Bet

    This is the simplest and most common form of esports bet. You predict which team or player will win the match. The odds are determined by the perceived strength of the teams or players involved, with favorites having lower odds and underdogs offering higher payouts.

    Example: In a CS:GO match between Team A and Team B, you bet on who will win the game.

  • Map Winner Bet

    In games like CS:GO, League of Legends, and Dota 2, matches are often played across multiple maps. You can place a bet on which team will win a specific map or series of maps.

    Example: In a CS:GO match, you bet on Team A to win Map 1.

  • Handicap Bet

    Similar to traditional sports, esports matches sometimes feature a handicap bet. This means one team is given a theoretical advantage or disadvantage to level the playing field. In esports, this might mean a team starts with a map advantage or needs to win by a certain number of kills or rounds.

    Example:Team A is given a -1.5 map handicap, meaning they need to win the series by 2 maps or more.

  • Over/Under (Total Rounds or Kills) Bet

    In an over/under bet, you wager on whether a specific statistic (like the total number of rounds in a CS:GO match or kills in a League of Legends game) will be over or under a number set by the sportsbook.

    Example:You bet that the total number of kills in a League of Legends game will be over 25.5.

  • Prop Bets (Proposition Bets)

    These bets are on specific occurrences during the game, such as whether a player will get a certain number of kills or if a specific event will take place. Prop bets add variety and excitement to the betting experience.

    Example: You place a bet on whether a particular CS:GO player will score more than 20 kills in a match.

  • Futures Bets

    Futures bets in esports are placed on long-term events, such as predicting the winner of a tournament before it begins. These bets are riskier because they require a prediction of future outcomes, but they offer high rewards if you pick correctly.

    Example: Betting on which team will win The International (Dota 2's annual championship) months before the event starts.

  • Live/In-Play Betting

    Live betting in esports allows you to place bets during a match, with the odds changing in real time as the action unfolds. This type of betting requires quick decisions and a deep understanding of the game’s pace and flow.

    Example: You place a bet on Team A to win after they score an early lead in a CS:GO round.

  • Accumulator (Parlay) Bets

    An accumulator bet combines multiple individual bets into one wager. All bets must win for the accumulator to payout, but the potential reward is greater due to the higher combined odds. This is a high-risk, high-reward betting option.

    Example:Betting on the winner of a CS:GO match, the first blood of a League of Legends game, and the MVP of a Dota 2 tournament, all in one parlay bet.

  • Outright Tournament Winner Bet

    This is a specific type of futures bet where you predict the winner of an entire esports tournament or league. Outright bets are typically placed before or during the tournament’s early stages.

    Example: Placing a bet on Team A to win the ESL Pro League before the tournament starts.

  • First Blood Bet

    In games like League of Legends or Dota 2, the first blood bet is about predicting which team will get the first kill of the match. This is an exciting and fast-paced bet that can be placed early in a game.

    Example: Betting on Team A to secure the first kill in a League of Legends match.

  • Total Kills/Points Bet

    Some sportsbooks offer bets on the total number of kills or points scored across multiple rounds or maps within a match. This is an exciting way to wager on a game’s overall momentum rather than just a match winner.

    Example: Betting that the total number of kills in a CS:GO match will be over or under 150.

  • Correct Score Bet

    A correct score bet is where you predict the exact score of a match or map. This type of bet can be more difficult but offers higher odds due to the accuracy required.

    Example:You bet that the final score of a CS:GO match will be 16-12 in favor of Team A.

  • Round Winner Bet

    In games like CS:GO or Rainbow Six Siege, you can bet on the outcome of a specific round. The round winner bet can apply to particular rounds, such as who will win the first round or a series of rounds.

    Example: You place a bet on Team A to win the first round in a CS:GO match.

  • Special Event Bets

    In addition to the usual match, map, and player bets, some sportsbooks offer special event bets. These might include unique scenarios that could happen during the event, such as specific strategies or unusual plays.

    Example:You bet on whether a team will secure an ace (killing all players from the opposing team) in a round of CS:GO.

Understanding Esports Betting Odds

Sports betting odds represent the probability of an event happening and determine how much you can win if your bet is successful. There are three main types of odds used in sports betting:

  • American Odds (Moneyline): These odds are commonly used in esports betting, especially in the United States. Positive odds represent the amount you can win from a $100 bet, while negative odds show how much you need to bet to win $100. For example, if a CS:GO team has +250 odds, a $100 bet would win you $250 if the team wins. Conversely, if the team has -150 odds, you would need to bet $150 to win $100.
  • Decimal Odds: Popular in Europe and Canada, decimal odds show the total payout for a winning bet, including the original stake. For example, odds of 2.50 mean you would receive $2.50 for every $1 wagered, including the original stake. In esports, this could apply to a bet on a Dota 2 team where odds of 2.50 indicate a payout of $250 for a $100 bet if the team wins.
  • Fractional Odds: Commonly used in the UK, fractional odds show how much you can win relative to your stake. For example, odds of 5/1 mean you can win $5 for every $1 wagered. In esports betting, such as on a Valorant match, a bet with odds of 5/1 would yield $5 for every $1 bet if your chosen team wins.
