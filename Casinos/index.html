Types of Online Casino Bets

Online casinos offer a variety of betting options, depending on the game you're playing. Here are some of the most common bet types:

Slot Bets

Slots are among the most popular casino games online. The bets in slots are typically determined by the number of paylines you choose to activate and the amount you wager per line.

  • Example 1: In a 5-reel, 20-payline slot game, you can bet $1 per line. If you activate all 20 paylines, your total bet will be $20 for one spin.

  • Example 2: In a progressive slot game, you might be required to place a minimum bet to qualify for the jackpot, such as $2 per spin. This ensures you’re in the running for the massive progressive jackpot.

Blackjack Bets

Blackjack offers various types of bets, with the primary goal being to beat the dealer by having a hand value closer to 21 than the dealer, without exceeding it. Here are some of the betting options:

  • Standard Bet:

    The basic wager placed at the start of a round.

    Example: Place a $10 bet on the table before the deal starts.

  • Insurance Bet:

    A side bet placed when the dealer shows an Ace as their upcard. This bet covers the possibility of the dealer having a blackjack.

    Example:Bet $5 on insurance if the dealer’s upcard is an Ace.

  • Double Down:

    You can double your initial bet after receiving your first two cards, but you are only allowed one additional card.

    Example: After receiving a 9 and 2, you choose to double down, increasing your bet to $20 for a chance to get one more card.

  • Split Bet:

    If you have two cards of the same rank, you can split them into two separate hands and place an additional bet on the second hand.

    Example: You receive two 8s. You split them into two separate hands, each with a $10 bet.

Roulette Bets

Roulette offers an array of betting options, which can be split into two categories: inside bets and outside bets.

  • Inside Bets:

    These are bets placed on individual numbers or small groups of numbers.

    Example 1 - Straight-up Bet :
    Bet on a single number, such as 17. If the ball lands on that number, you win 35:1 on your bet.

    Example 2 - Split Bet:
    Bet on two adjacent numbers by placing your chip on the line between them. If either number hits, you win 17:1 on your bet.

    Example 3 - Street Bet:
    Bet on a row of three consecutive numbers. If any of these numbers hit, you win 11:1.

  • Outside Bets:

    These are bets placed on larger groups of numbers or characteristics like color or odd/even.

    Example 1 - Red or Black Bet: Bet on the color of the winning number. If it lands on red, you win your bet at 1:1 odds.

    Example 2 - Odd or Even Bet: Bet that the winning number will be odd or even. If it matches, you win at 1:1 odds.

    Example 3 - Dozen Bet: Bet on a set of 12 numbers (1-12, 13-24, 25-36). If the winning number falls within your chosen dozen, you win 2:1.

Poker Bets

Poker games, like Texas Hold'em and Omaha, offer various betting structures. These bets can range from forced bets (like blinds) to strategic decisions made during the course of the hand.

  • Ante:

    A forced bet that all players must place before the hand starts.

    Example: In a $1/$2 Texas Hold'em game, players are required to place a $1 small blind and a $2 big blind before the cards are dealt.

  • Blinds:

    In some poker games (like Texas Hold'em and Omaha), players are required to place forced bets before the hand begins. These are the small blind and big blind.

    Example: In a $1/$2 game, the small blind is $1, and the big blind is $2.

  • Calls:

    Matching the current bet or raise.

    Example: If an opponent raises the bet to $10, you can call by placing a $10 bet to stay in the hand.

  • Raise:

    Increasing the current bet.

    Example: If the initial bet is $10, you can raise to $20 to increase the wager on the hand.

  • Check:

    Passing the action to the next player without betting, typically done when no bet has been placed in the round.

    Example: If no one has placed a bet in a round, you can check to pass the action without committing any money to the pot.

Craps Bets

Craps is a fast-paced dice game with a variety of betting options, all based on the outcome of the dice roll.

  • Pass Line Bet:

    A basic bet that the shooter will roll a 7 or 11 on the come-out roll or establish a point and then hit that point again before rolling a 7.

    Example: Bet $5 on the pass line. If the shooter rolls a 7 or 11 on the come-out roll, you win your bet at 1:1 odds.

  • Don’t Pass Bet:

    The opposite of the pass line bet. You’re betting that the shooter will roll a 2, 3, or 12 on the come-out roll or establish a point and roll a 7 before hitting the point again.

    Example: Bet $5 on the don’t pass line. If the shooter rolls a 2, 3, or 12, you win your bet.

  • Come Bet:

    Similar to the pass line bet but made after a point is established. You’re betting that the next roll will be 7 or 11, or that a point will be established and then hit again.

    Example: After a point has been established, you place a $5 come bet. If the next roll is a 7 or 11, you win your bet.

  • Place Bet:

    Bet that a specific number (4, 5, 6, 8, 9, or 10) will be rolled before a 7.

    Example: Place a $5 bet on 8. If an 8 is rolled before a 7, you win at 7:6 odds.
